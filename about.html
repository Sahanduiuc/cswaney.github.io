Research

A Limit Order Book Factor Model

The Information Content of Limit Order Books (Working Paper).

In this paper I develop a factor model of limit order book dynamics. I analyze a unique dataset of limit order book snapshots reconstructed from nanosecond timestamped messages from the NASDAQ exchange. I find that the limit order book is characterized by three familiar factors in the finance literature: level, slope and curvature. Not only are these features found to characterize limit order books, but they also correlate with changes in the order book midprice over one-minute time intervals. The results help explain the success of several other limit order book factors proposed in the literature.

Fund Manager Skill, Mixture Models, and Expectation Maximization

Evaluating Fund Manager Skill: A Mixture Model Approach (Working Paper).

From the average investor’s perspective, the performance of actively managed mutual funds is among the most important topics in the field of finance. In this paper I present a new analysis of activtely-managed funds based on mixture modeling and expectation maximization estimation. The analysis accounts for the likelihood of false positives in the multiple regresssion setting. Analyzing the performance of mutual funds over nearly thirty years, I find that the data consistent with the assumption that a small subpopulation of talented funds. Consistent with the prior literature, the proposed methodology is unable to identify these talented funds on a consistent basis.

Efficient Skin Segmentation via Neural Networks: HP-ELM and BD-SOM

Efficient Skin Segmentation via Neural Networks: HP-ELM and BD-SOM, Procedia Computer Science, 2015.

Self-organizing maps are unsupervised neural networks. In this paper we develop a self-organizing map algorithm suitable for classification tasks invovling Big Data. Our implementation takes advantage of GPU acceleration to simultaneously classify hundreds of thousands of observations. The algorithm achieves near state-of-the-art performance on a pixel-level skin-segmentation task.
